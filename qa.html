0 What are the requirements for tourists visa? The requirements for a tourist visa through Attic Tours are as follows:\n\n1. **Passport** (Holder’s signature required)\n2. **Visa Application Form** (A facial Photo (4.5×3.5cm) must be attached)\n3. **PSA issued Birth Certificate** (issued within 1 year) and **Marriage Certificate** (for married applicants) - Unnecessary if there is a used Japan Visa on passport.\n - If the birth certificate is unreadable, submit a certificate issued by the Local Civil Registrar.\n - If the birth certificate is a "LATE REGISTRATION," submit a Baptismal Certificate and School Record (Form 137).\n - If there is no record of birth in PSA, submit a Birth Certificate from the Local Civil Registrar and a Negative Certificate from PSA.\n4. **Itinerary in Japan**\n5. **Bank Certificate** (balance within the last six months must be shown)\n - If the Average Daily Balance (ADB) is not indicated, a bank statement must be submitted.\n6. **Tax Payment Certificate** (Form 2316) - must be signed by both employer and employee.\n - For business owners, proof of actual payment of tax must be submitted.\n7. **Employment Certificate** (must indicate period of employment, salary, and position)\n - For business owners, submit a Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) “Certificate of Business Name Registration” and Mayor’s Permit.\n - If unable to submit the tax and employment certificates, a letter of explanation must be provided.\n8. **Guarantee Letter** (if a guarantor in the Philippines is covering part/all of travel expenses)\n9. **Proof of relationship** between applicant and guarantor (e.g., Birth Certificates or Photos)\n10. **Photocopy of Tax Payment Certificate** and **Employment Certificate** of the guarantor.\n\nMake sure to check with the Attic Tours for any updates or changes in requirements. For further information, you can visit their office or contact them directly.
1 What are the difference between tourists and visit relative or friend visa? The primary differences between a tourist visa and a visiting relatives or friends visa for Japan, as outlined by Attic Tours, are as follows:\n\n1. **Purpose of Visit:**\n - **Tourist Visa:** The purpose is to visit Japan for tourism, sightseeing, or leisure activities.\n - **Visiting Relatives or Friends Visa:** The purpose is to visit friends or relatives who reside in Japan.\n\n2. **Requirements:**\n - **Tourist Visa:** Generally requires a passport, application form, facial photo, birth certificate, marriage certificate (if applicable), and an itinerary in Japan.\n - **Visiting Relatives or Friends Visa:** Requires similar documents but also includes an invitation letter from the friend or relative in Japan, proof of relationship, residence certificate of the inviter, and possibly additional financial documents if the inviter is covering expenses.\n\n3. **Proof of Relationship:**\n - **Tourist Visa:** No proof of relationship is necessary since the visit is for tourism.\n - **Visiting Relatives or Friends Visa:** Requires documentation to prove the relationship with the inviter, such as birth or marriage certificates, photos, or correspondence.\n\n4. **Financial Requirements:**\n - **Tourist Visa:** May require bank certificates and tax payment certificates to demonstrate the applicant’s ability to support themselves during their stay.\n - **Visiting Relatives or Friends Visa:** May require similar financial documents for both the applicant and the inviter if the inviter is covering expenses.\n\n5. **Application Process:**\n - **Tourist Visa:** The application process is more straightforward and typically does not require as many supporting documents related to relationships.\n - **Visiting Relatives or Friends Visa:** The process is more detailed due to the need for additional documentation to establish relationships and intentions.\n\nFor more detailed information about specific requirements, you can visit the official website of the Japanese Embassy in the Philippines: [Japan Visa Information](https://www.ph.emb-japan.go.jp/itpr_en/00_000035.html).

6 What are the requirements for visiting relatives? The requirements for visiting relatives in Japan include the following:\n\n**APPLICANT’S REQUIREMENTS:**\n1. **Passport:** Holder’s signature required.\n2. **Application Form:** A facial photo (4.5×3.5cm) must be attached. Submit all relatives’ Birth Certificate/Marriage Certificate enough to prove the third-degree relationship between Applicant and Inviter.\n3. **Birth Certificate:** Issued by PSA within 1 year.\n - Additional Requirements:\n - If unreadable, submit a Birth Certificate issued by Local Civil Registrar.\n - If “LATE REGISTRATION,” submit Baptismal Certificate and School Record (DepEd: Form 137).\n - If no record of birth in PSA, submit Birth Certificate issued by Local Civil Registrar and Negative Certificate issued by PSA.\n4. **Marriage Certificate:** Issued by PSA within 1 year (for married applicants only).\n - Additional Requirements:\n - If unreadable, submit Marriage Certificate issued by Local Civil Registrar.\n - If no record of marriage in PSA, submit Marriage Certificate issued by Local Civil Registrar and Negative Certificate issued by PSA.\n5. **Bank Certificate:** If the applicant will shoulder part/all of travel expenses.\n6. **Photocopy of Income Tax Return (BIR Form):** If the applicant will shoulder part/all of travel expenses.\n\n**INVIITER’S REQUIREMENTS:**\n7. **Invitation Letter.**\n8. **Itinerary in Japan.**\n9. **Residence Certificate (Jumin-hyo):** Must describe all household members. If the inviter or spouse is Japanese, submit Koseki-tohon. If not Japanese, submit a photocopy of Residence Card (both sides).\n10. **Guarantee Letter:** If the guarantor will shoulder part/all of travel expenses.\n11. **Latest Financial Documents:** To show guarantor’s financial capacity (options include Income Certificate, Tax Certificate, Photocopy of Final Tax Return Form, or Bank Certificate).\n\nFor more detailed information, you can visit the [Japan Visa Information](https://www.ph.emb-japan.go.jp/itpr_en/00_000035.html) page.
